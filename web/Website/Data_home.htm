The NLFA - Overview

The Natinal Listing of Fish Advisories (NLFA) is a database that contains information about the species and size of fish under advisory, the chemical contaminants causing the advisory, the location of the water body, and the population for whom the advisory was issued.

The inclusion of information provided by states, tribes, territories and Canada does not imply any official EPA endorsement. Furthermore, EPA does not accept any responsibility for the data provided by these entities or guarantee the validity of this data.

Defining an Advisory

Fish consumption advisories warn the public that high concentrations of chemical contaminants have been found or are suspected in fish from local waters and that consumption of these fish may pose health risks. The advisories may recommend to limit or avoid consumption of specific fish species or to limit or avoid consumption of fish from specific waterbodies.

It is important to understand that while a single advisory has one geographic location (e.g. segment of a river or lake) it can contain information about several fish species (e.g. carp and largemouth bass), several pollutants (e.g. mercury and PCB's), and several "populations" (e.g. no consumption for at risk subpopulations such as pregnant women and restricted consumption for general populations).

Federal Advisories

The national advisory includes advice on eating fish caught by family and friends from local waters. The national advice says that in the absence of local advice, you can eat up to 6 ounces (one average meal) per week of fish you catch from local waters, but do not consume any other fish during that week. This technical memorandum documents the derivation of the 6 ounces per week. Technical Memorandum: Origin of 1 meal / week Freshwater Fish Consumption Rate (PDF) (84K, 5 pp.)

Getting Started

Welcome to the on-line version of the National Listing of Fish Advisories (NLFA). This web site allows you to select an advisory (or set of advisories) of interest to you and then obtain detailed textual information about these advisories as well as map these advisories.

If you receive a "Server Busy" message hit the back button on your browser and try again. If you continue to get the "Server
Busy" it is an indication that the site may be busy generating complex maps for other users and you may want to access the site again at a later time.

Getting Help:
Every web page that makes up the NLFA web site has help information that gives a short overview of that page and describes all of the options available on that page. Simply click on the "Help" button on the main advisory information page to get to this help information.

Finding an advisory where you live:
Generally, advisories can be selected in the following 3 ways:
1. Zoom in to a specific advisory using the map and then identify it.
2. Choose an advisory by name (typically a waterbody name) from the drop-down list provided.
3. Set up your own selection criteria (e.g. "show me all active advisories for chlordane in WI") and run a database "query"
Click on "Help" from the main Advisory Information page to get more detailed help on how to select advisories.

Other Functions:
The on-line version of the NLFA also allows you to:
1. Count all advisories meeting your selection criteria by state
2. See detailed fish tissue data (often used to determine when an advisory is or isn't necessary) that meets your selection critieria.
